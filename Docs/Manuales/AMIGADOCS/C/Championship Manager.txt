NEW GAME/LOADING A SAVED GAME

The next menu gives you the chance to start a completely new game or load a
previously saved game.

1. NEW GAME

You will be asked how many players are to participate (maximum four).
Each then chooses a club (click on a club name), his/?her personality
and second name. Next, you are asked to inseret a blank disk,
(see Formating a Blank Disk).  This disk is known as the Season Disk
since it will contain data relevant to the season currently being 
played.
If you insert a previously used season disk, the previous season data
will be erased (see Main Menu).

2. LOAD SAVED GAME

Insert your season disk as requested. Play resumes at the main menu
(see The Main Menu).

3. FORMATING A BLANK DISK TO SAVE A GAME

To create a saved game you will need to format a blank disk,to do this
follow the instructions below.

Amiga

Insert Workbench disk DFO. When loaded eject disk and insert a blank
disk in DFO,click on DFO disk then click on menu bar with right mouse
button to highlight Disk, then highlight Initialize on the pull down
menu.      

Atari ST

Insert blank disk in drive A,from the TOS scren click on floppy disk
A. Move the pointer onto File and click on OK. Select double sided and 
click on format.

PAGE 18 BUYING A PLAYER

APPROACHING 

Click on BUY to approach the player. You will then be informed how much 
money you have availableand asked to confirm that you want to approach.

The club then decides if it is willing to release him, and if it is the
player decides if he wants to join you.

If the player is at a human-cnotrolled club, a menu will appear allowing 
you to accept or refuse the approach.

You cannot refuse an approach to a player who is out of contract.

OTHER BIDDERS

Any other clubs who have the player on their shortlist are given a 
chance to join in the bidding.In case of Human-controlled clubs, a 
menu appear giving you the chance to approach the player. 
If he is interested,then you will be able to bid for the player
 
NEGOTIATING THE FEE

Assuming, the player is not on a free transfer, the clubs then agree
a fee for the player.
Firstly each of the bidding clubs makes an offer.
Secondly, the selling clubs sets an asking price

The pocess is repeated until:-
a)A fee is agreed.
b)No agreement is reached after several rounds of negotiations.
  If no fee has been agreed and the player is out of contract, the 
  inderpendent tribunal set the fee.
